Simple Mantra Meditation

Everyone can meditate. It doesn't have anything to do with religion or spirituality, it's simply a tool to help you think more clearly and to calm your monkey mind. On this site, you'll find a simple step-by-step guide to mantra-based meditation.

How to Meditate

At its core, meditation is simply about abstaining from conscious mental activity. This is usually done by focusing on your breathing or on a mantra. Every time a thought pops into your mind, you calmly direct your attention back to your focus.

There are many schools of thought around this, but on this site, we'll teach you a simple mantra-based meditation.

Your Mantra

A mantra is a simple meaningless word that you repeat internally in your head.

The key idea is to not associate the mantra with anything. That way it doesn't spur any thoughts or feelings while you meditate.

To keep it neutral, you will be given a mantra. Avoid sharing your mantra with others or saying it out loud.

The Protocol

1. Find a place to sit.

Find a quiet space where you can sit uninterrupted for 20 minutes. Make sure you have a stopwatch or clock nearby so you can track your time.

2. Close your eyes and get comfortable.

In the start, your body might feel itchy or uncomfortable. Take time to get comfortable; this is especially important.

3. Repeat your mantra internally for 15 minutes.

There is no correct rhythm or speed; simply repeat your mantra in a way that feels natural to you. It might get louder or quieter as you meditate; that's very normal. If a thought pops into your head, calmly direct your attention back to your mantra.

4. Let your mind wander.

Once the 15 minutes are over, stop repeating the mantra and let your mind relax for 3-4 minutes. Feel free to lay down and let your mind wander during this time.

Repeat this protocol two times a day. Once in the morning and once in the evening.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: I feel really restless while meditating. What should I do?

A: This will happen; just keep meditating. If it's particularly irritating, feel free to stop repeating the mantra and focus your attention on the sensations for a while as you lean into them.

Q: I keep falling asleep while meditating. What should I do?

A: Get more sleep.

Q: How long does it take before I start feeling any effects?

A: Some people feel a clear shift happening after doing two sessions a day for a week or more.
